Duality Project - Arduino x AI

Exploring the Intersection of Emotions, Physiology, and Artificial Intelligence

Project Overview

In this project, an Arduino circuit measures the user's heart rate by detecting blood flow in the fingertip. This data is recorded and used to calculate the user's beats per minute (bpm), providing an objective measure of their emotional state.

HumeAI utilises advanced facial recognition technology to analyse emotions. Users initiate the process by capturing an image via their webcam and indicating their readiness by pressing the 'C' key. HumeAI then employs natural language processing and complex algorithms to identify emotional indicators, listing the top three emotions based on facial cues.

Objective and Research

The project aims to explore the relationship between physiological indicators and perceived emotions, combining data from the Arduino and HumeAI. This reveals variations between physiological and emotional responses, emphasising the integral role of emotions in intelligence (Mart√≠nez-Miranda et al., 2005).

Philosophically, this project delves into the debate between Physicalists and Dualists, questioning the mind's nature and whether machines can genuinely experience consciousness and emotions, as raised by scholars like Nussbaum (2003) and Descartes (1954).

Key Technologies

This project integrates technology with physiological aspects, using a pulse sensor and Arduino to measure heart rate. On the AI side, facial recognition with HumeAI analyses emotional cues, providing both objective and subjective insights into emotional states.

Despite AI's capability to decode physical attributes, it struggles with understanding the authenticity of human emotions, as highlighted by John Searle's "Chinese Room" thought experiment (Searle, 1980).

Ethical Considerations

The project raises important ethical concerns, such as the responsibility and accountability of AI systems in interpreting human emotions. Instances where physiological responses contradict AI assessments underscore the need for nuanced understanding of emotional intelligence.

Research by scholars like Rajpurkar et al. (2022) reinforces the critical role of human involvement in areas that demand emotional depth and authenticity, particularly in sensitive contexts like healthcare.
