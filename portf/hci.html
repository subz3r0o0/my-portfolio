The Axis Plus Controller

Human Computer Interaction (HCI) Project - Assistive Technology for Gamers

Project Overview

Refined precision and accuracy are significant factors for exceeding performance during gameplay. The importance of accuracy varies for players, whether to avoid mistakes, eliminate the opponent, or win the game. However, an often-overlooked challenge is the burden some gamers face using a standard handheld controller, which requires ambidextrous finger manipulations for simultaneous inputs. For gamers facing mobility, dexterity, or stamina limitations, interacting with a video game can be a significant challenge (Maggiorini, 2017).

Research suggests that 2% of the US population cannot play video games due to disability, and 9% of disabled gamers can only experience limited gameplay (Yuan et al, 2010). The Axis+ controller was developed to fill the shortage of assistive controller options, providing a better, more comfortable, and engaging experience for disabled gamers.

Axis Plus Controller: Key Features

The Axis Plus controller specializes as a functional assistive controller for any game that uses traditional controls. Users can toggle between standard buttons and sensor-based controls based on their preference. This flexibility is tailored for gamers with impaired motor skills who wish to play games comfortably with minimal contact, widening access to the gaming community. The controller's USB-A connectivity ensures compatibility with both eighth/ninth-generation consoles and modern personal computers, aiming to open the gaming experience to new players.
