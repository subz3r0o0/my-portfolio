SODI - Socratic Odyssey to Digital Inclusion

Graduate Project - Exploring the Digital Divide Through Socratic Dialogue

Purpose of SODI

In this era of rapid digital advancement, characterised by Industry 4.0 devices such as blockchain, artificial intelligence, and big data, the gap between technology's potential and accessibility is increasingly evident. SODI is an installation designed to raise awareness about the digital divide. It features an 'Agora' box where users respond to Socratic prompts, writing their insights on cards. These cards symbolise users' contributions towards bridging the digital divide, engaging participants in reflective and collaborative discussions.

The SODI project draws inspiration from the Socratic method, encouraging an environment of inquiry and dialogue. Socrates emphasised the importance of asking questions and sharing knowledge. By encouraging users to engage thoughtfully with technology, SODI aims to promote digital inclusion and collaborative learning.

SODI's Objective

The objective of the SODI project is to create an engaging and educational tool that helps users learn and interact with digital technologies in a more inclusive manner. By following the guided steps, users can capture images, input data, and contribute to a digital knowledge-sharing platform.

How to Use SODI

Users follow the website's step-by-step guide, which involves the following actions:

  • Take a wooden card and write an answer to one of the Socratic prompts visible on the Agora box.
  • Place the wooden card on the Agora box.
  • Click the 'Capture Image' button to capture an image, which triggers a sound and lights up a neopixel. The contribution is then displayed on the website.
  • Insert the card into the box, triggering a green neopixel light as confirmation that the contribution has been saved.
